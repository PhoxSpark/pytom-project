Pytom (Python + Atom)

As I try to clarify with his name, Pytom is a program made for actually download an organism file (PDB) and look for concrete atoms. For example, take the 2KI5 organism from PDB databases and search the information of all his carbon atoms. This is not so useful at a glance, but the objective is to learn how to work with frameworks, Git and public repositories.

Of course the code can be improved with new functions or with better code (I'm not a master, you know), and maybe I will take it once finished for adding more features, but at this moment the main objective is clear: Take information of concrete atoms from concrete organisms.

What I'm seeing?

What you are actually seeing is a WebApp made with the microframework Flask. You may know it or even use it, but for me is all new and exciting! In the future I'm looking to learn how to use proper frameworks like Django or so, but for this project flask is enugh (actually I'm falling in love with it!).
